Types of Cuisines

  1. Asian Cuisine

    Asian cuisine refers to the variety of cooking styles, ingredients, and food traditions found across the continent of Asia — the largest and most culturally diverse region on Earth.

    Because Asia is so vast, its cuisines differ greatly from region to region — from the spicy curries of India to the light and balanced meals of Japan.

  2. European Cuisine

    European cuisine refers to the traditional foods, ingredients, and cooking styles that originated in Europe.

    It includes both simple rustic meals (like bread, cheese, and soups) and elegant fine dining (like French pastries or Italian pasta).

    European food has also shaped Western cooking worldwide — influencing restaurants, bakeries, and kitchens across the globe.

  3. American Cuisine

    American cuisine refers to the food traditions and cooking styles found in the United States and the American continents (North, Central, and South America).

    It’s a mix of Native American roots, immigrant influences, and regional creativity — making it one of the most varied cuisines anywhere on Earth.

  4. Middle Eastern & African Cuisine

    Middle Eastern cuisine includes foods from countries like Lebanon, Turkey, Iran, Israel, Saudi Arabia, Jordan, Egypt, and Syria.

    It’s known for its fragrant spices, grilled meats, fresh herbs, and shared dishes.

    Meals are often served family-style — with mezze (small plates), bread, and dips.
