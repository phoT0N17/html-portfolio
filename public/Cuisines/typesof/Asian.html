Types of Asian Cuisines:

  1. Indian Cuisine

    Key features: Bold spices, complex flavors, vegetarian-friendly, and regionally diverse.

    Common Ingredients: Rice, lentils, spices (turmeric, cumin, coriander, garam masala), ghee, and yogurt.

    Popular Dishes: Butter Chicken, Biryani, Paneer Tikka, Dosa and Idli (South India), Rogan Josh (North India)

    Regional Styles

    • North India: Rich curries and breads (naan, roti)
    • South India: Rice-based and coconut-flavored dishes
    • East India: Sweet and spicy (Gujarati thali, Goan seafood)
    • West India: Mustard oil and fish (Bengali cuisine)

  2. Chinese Cuisine

    Key features: Balance of flavors (sweet, sour, salty, bitter, umami), colorful presentation, and variety of textures.

    Common Ingredients: Soy sauce, rice, noodles, tofu, garlic, ginger, sesame oil.

    Popular Dishes: Fried Rice, Dumplings, Kung Pao Chicken, Sweet and Sour Pork, Spring Rolls

    Regional Styles

    • Cantonese: Rich curries and breads (naan, roti)
    • Schezwan: Rice-based and coconut-flavored dishes
    • Hunan: Sweet and spicy (Gujarati thali, Goan seafood)
    • Northern (Beijing): Mustard oil and fish (Bengali cuisine)

  3. Japanese Cuisine

    Key features: Fresh, simple, and focused on natural flavor and presentation.

    Common Ingredients: Rice, soy sauce, fish, seaweed, miso, tofu.

    Popular Dishes: Sushi, Ramen, Tempura, Teriyaki, Miso Soup

    Cultural Note: Japanese meals emphasize seasonality, balance, and visual beauty
